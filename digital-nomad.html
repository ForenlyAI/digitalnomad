Digital Nomad Starter Kit

Tools, mindset, and lessons from working remotely across different countries.

The Mindset Shift

Being a digital nomad isn't just about working from beaches. It's about designing a life where location is a choice, not a constraint. The real challenge isn't logistics—it's discipline.

Essential Gear

  • Laptop: MacBook Air M2 - lightweight, powerful, all-day battery.
  • Hotspot: Always have a backup internet solution.
  • Noise-cancelling headphones: Essential for cafés and co-working spaces.
  • Travel adapter: Universal USB-C adapter covers most countries.
  • Portable charger: 20,000mAh minimum for long travel days.

Choosing Destinations

Look for: reliable internet, affordable cost of living, good timezone overlap with clients, and a community of other remote workers. Popular spots: Lisbon, Bali, Mexico City, Istanbul.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Moving too fast - give each place at least 2-4 weeks.
  • Underestimating timezone challenges for calls.
  • Not having travel insurance (get SafetyWing or World Nomads).
  • Forgetting to build local routines and connections.
