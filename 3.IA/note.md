This document outlines the professional titles, abbreviations, and strategic positioning for working as a "Sales/Investment Consultant" in high-end markets like London-based **API Global**, while operating as a digital nomad.

## üèõÔ∏è Professional Titles & Abbreviations

In the international business world (especially the UK), titles can be written in full or abbreviated. Some are professional certifications, while others describe organizational roles.

### 1. General Financial Abbreviations
| Abbreviation | Full Title | Usage Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **FA** | Financial Advisor | Most common, general financial planning. |
| **IA** | Investment Advisor | Specifically focused on capital allocation/investments. |
| **IC** | Investment Consultant | Often used for project-based or corporate consulting. |
| **IFA** | Independent Financial Adviser | Highly prestigious in the UK/EU (requires specific licensing). |
| **RIA** | Registered Investment Advisor | Primarily US-based regulatory term. |

### 2. Wealth & Management Focused
| Abbreviation | Full Title | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **WM** | Wealth Manager | Manages total portfolio for High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWI). |
| **AM** | Asset Manager | Manages specific assets (Property, stocks, funds). |

### 3. Sales & Business Development (The "Sales Guy" Roles)
| Abbreviation | Full Title | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **BDM** | Business Development Manager | **Standard for growth & sales.** Focuses on expanding the investor network and closing deals. |
| **BD** | Business Development | General department or broader role name. |

## üè¢ API Global & Property Investment Context

For property-focused investment companies (like API Global), specific titles carry more weight and accurately describe the service:

*   **PIC (Property Investment Consultant):** Specific to real estate investment firms.
*   **REIA (Real Estate Investment Advisor):** Focused on property-based investment strategies.
*   **Investment Consultant:** A professional middle ground that sounds advisory rather than purely "sales."
